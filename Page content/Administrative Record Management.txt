Overview
This course equips learners with the skills to manage administrative records effectively, ensuring confidentiality, accuracy, and compliance with organisational policies.

Description
The Manage Administration Records course provides a structured approach to handling confidential information, securing documents, and implementing effective record-keeping systems. You will learn to develop and control office stationery plans, monitor stock levels, and apply shrinkage control measures. Additionally, the course covers procedures for identifying and reporting non-conformances, ensuring compliance with company policies. By the end of the course, you will be able to maintain organised and secure administrative records that support smooth business operations.

Unit 1: Control and deal with confidential information and documents
1. Confidential information is defined and described for a specific organisation. 2. The system and procedures used for dealing with confidential information is explained with examples. 3. Documents are secured in an appropriate manner and within an agreed time frame. 4. Utmost care is taken to avoid compromising any relevant stakeholder.

Unit 2: Control and evaluate ordering and distribution of office stationery
1. A plan is presented to effectively control office stationery policies, procedures, and strategies. 2. Plans are developed on how to monitor, maintain, and improve stock levels in accordance with organisational requirements. 3. Effective shrinkage controls are developed and implemented for an organisation. 4. Evidence of accurate reports is produced reflecting stationery movements for a given period. 5. The control of the administration system and procedures is explained and applied to ensure effective administering of stationery. 6. Documentation used for the control of stationery in an accurate, complete way, which complies with requirements is explained to employees. 7. Effective stationery control procedures, systems, and follow-up actions are evaluated and explained.

Unit 3: Implement control measures with individuals
1. Contracted control measures are implemented and non-conformances are identified for reporting to persons with the authority to deal with them. 2. Non-conformances are recorded and corrective actions negotiated according to organisational requirements. 3. Reported non-conformance is handled in accordance with organisational policies and procedures and is duly recorded.
