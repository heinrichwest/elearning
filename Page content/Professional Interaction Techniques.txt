Overview
Professional behaviour is the cornerstone of a productive and respectful workplace. This course empowers individuals to present themselves with confidence, communicate effectively, and uphold workplace standards that reflect integrity, reliability, and mutual respect.

Description
This course explores the expectations and standards of professional conduct within business environments. Learners will develop awareness of appropriate dress, communication styles, non-verbal signals, customer interaction, and ethical boundariesâ€”including issues like confidentiality and harassment. Through practical observation, demonstration, and reflection, participants will assess their own behaviour, improve interpersonal effectiveness, and respond professionally to various workplace challenges. The course also supports self-development by guiding learners in identifying personal growth areas aligned with company standards and performance goals.

Unit 1: Know how to behave in a business environment
1. The dress code of an organisation is described, and an indication is given of what is regarded as acceptable and unacceptable dress in a particular work environment 2. The behaviour of three peers in a business workplace is observed and assessed against the recognised parameters of appropriate behaviour in a specific professional environment 3. Positive listening skills are demonstrated, and an indication is given of the potential consequences of displaying negative listening skills 4. Regulations regarding smoking in the workplace are explained with reference to both legal requirements and established policy 5. A Company Code of Good Conduct is understood and explained, and an indication is given of the consequences of non-compliance 6. The concept of a work ethic is explained with reference to the responsibility and accountability of the individual

Unit 2: Interpret body language in a business context
1. Non-verbal communication signals are interpreted, and an indication is given of how body language can reinforce or contradict a verbal message 2. The role of posture and eye contact in communication is explained with examples from a business context 3. The use of a handshake as a greeting in a business setting is demonstrated, and the meanings of different handshakes used in South Africa are compared with examples of when it is appropriate to use each handshake 4. The concept of personal space is explained, and an indication is given of the consequences if one ignores commonly accepted business behaviour 5. Behaviour that could be considered as sexual harassment in the workplace is described, and an indication is given of how to deal with instances of sexual harassment

Unit 3: Interact with people in a business setting
1. The importance of first impressions in a business environment is explained, and an indication is given of how first impressions can determine subsequent behaviour 2. Visitors are greeted in keeping with established organisational culture 3. People are introduced to each other in a business-like manner 4. Ways of closing an interaction with a visitor or customer are demonstrated for three different scenarios 5. The importance of delivering on promises or making alternative arrangements is explained with reference to good customer service 6. Ways of behaving professionally when provoked or in difficult circumstances are demonstrated for three different scenarios 7. The importance of confidentiality in interpersonal relationships is explained with reference to company policy

Unit 4: Assess own professional behaviour in a business setting
1. Personal behaviour is analysed in three different situations in a business environment 2. Personal behaviour is measured against own performance agreement or company standards 3. Self-development needs are identified, and a plan is drawn up to address those needs
