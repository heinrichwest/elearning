Description
This course equips learners with the skills and understanding necessary to effectively conduct technical practitioner meetings within a professional context. Participants will explore various types of technical meetings, leadership styles, decision-making processes, and essential meeting conventions. From preparation through to post-meeting follow-up, learners will be guided through every phase of the process, including setting agendas, encouraging active participation, managing conflict, and producing accurate records. Emphasis is placed on professional protocols, effective communication, and collaborative decision-making tailored to the technical field. By the end of the course, learners will be capable of chairing and contributing to structured, outcome-driven meetings that align with organisational 
policies and objectives.


Unit 1: Demonstrate knowledge of different types of technical practitioners meetings
1. The demonstration describes the types of technical meetings and their uses. 2. The demonstration identifies leadership styles used in meeting procedures. 3. The demonstration identifies decision-making processes used in meetings. 4. The demonstration describes meeting conventions relevant to the type of meeting chosen. 5. The demonstration identifies that the note-taker should have technical background knowledge.
Unit 2: Prepare for a technical practitioners meeting
1. The preparation ensures physical arrangements for the meeting are done, and relevant to the type of meeting. 2. The preparation ensures that meeting outcomes are clear, concise, and well-documented. 3. The preparation ensures that meeting invitations are extended to relevant participants timeously. 4. The preparation completes and distributes the meeting agenda and other supporting documentation needed for the type of meeting.

Unit 3: Chair a technical practitioners meeting
1. The chair and members agree on rules and guidelines for behaviour. 2. The chair applies agreed meeting conventions throughout the meeting, according to the type of meeting and in accordance with the standing procedures of the organisation(s) involved. 3. The chair ensures that the published agenda is followed. 4. The chair provides for active participation by all members to avoid/minimise conflict. 5. The chair ensures that meeting topics are prioritised and that discussion times are allocated according to importance, urgency, complexity, and agenda. 6. The chair ensures that agreed decisions are clear, and accurate, include a time frame for action, and are within the mandate of the type of meeting conducted.

Unit 4: Conduct post meeting follow up for a technical meeting
1. The follow-up ensures minutes of the meeting are produced accurately and in line with the policy of the organisation. 2. The follow-up communicates agreed records of discussion to interested parties in a format and time frame that meet the requirements of the type of meeting and the organisation(s) involved. 3. The follow-up includes a summary of discussions and actions and meet format requirements.
