Overview
Understanding corporate sales requires a blend of research, strategic communication, and financial insight. This course equips learners with the knowledge and skills to identify corporate prospects, engage decision-makers, present tailored solutions, and conclude deals efficiently and ethically, in line with company policies and procedures.

Description
This course provides a comprehensive framework for managing the corporate sales process from lead identification to deal closure. Learners will explore techniques to research and compile potential prospects, communicate effectively with clients, and present purchasing options that meet financial and operational requirements. By the end of this course, learners will be capable of managing the full corporate sales cycle, from prospect research and engagement to presenting solutions and closing deals, with professionalism, accuracy, and adherence to company standards.


Unit 1: Source new corporate prospects
1. The objectives for a corporate interface are explained with reference to company policy and procedures. 2. Corporate prospects in a specific area are identified from referred and non-referred leads. 3. Methods to research source leads are described with reference to the identification of related corporate prospects. 4. A database of potential corporate prospects is compiled to inform decisions. 5. Source leads are listed with reference to possible corporate clients.

Unit 2: Make contact with corporate prospects
1. The most viable purchasing options are established in terms of the corporate prospects' financial position and in accordance with company procedures. 2. Different methods of contacting corporate prospects are explained in terms of pros and cons and research done on the prospective client. 3. Company policies and procedures secure an appointment with the decision-makers. Information is recorded with reference to the requirements and/or present status of the corporate fleet. 4. Company policy and procedures present information on the organisation and products relevant to the corporate prospect's requirements. 5. The financial position of a corporate prospect is established in terms of the envisaged sale and in accordance with company policies and procedures. 6. Decision-makers of prospective clients are identified prior to a sales intervention with reference to who can make a purchasing decision.

Unit 4: Obtain necessary approval to conclude the deal
1. Obtain necessary approval to conclude the deal. 2. Proof of approval for the deal is presented in terms of obtaining approval from the prospective customer and own management. 3. The terms and conditions for further dealings are compiled according to company policy and procedures. 4. Agreement is obtained from the prospective customer to conclude the deal.
