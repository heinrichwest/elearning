Overview
Learn to give evidence in court confidently and effectively with this comprehensive course. Ideal for legal professionals, law enforcement officers, and expert witnesses, the course covers courtroom procedures, witness preparation, delivering testimony, and handling cross-examination. Gain the skills to present clear, credible, and persuasive evidence in legal proceedings.

Description
Master the art of giving evidence in court with our detailed course, "Give Evidence in Court." This course is tailored for legal professionals, law enforcement officers, expert witnesses, and anyone required to present evidence in legal proceedings. Develop the skills and confidence to deliver clear, credible, and persuasive testimony in a courtroom setting. With expert guidance and practical exercises, this course equips you with the knowledge and confidence to give evidence in court effectively. Whether you're new to the courtroom or seeking to enhance your testimony skills, "Give Evidence in Court" provides the essential training to ensure your evidence is clear, credible, and compelling.


Study Unit 1: Courts of law in South Africa
1. Introduction. 2. General Principles of the South African Judiciary. 3. Court Structure in South Africa. 4. Typical Layout of a Court. 5. The People who Make Courts Function. 6. Obtaining Legal Representation


Study Unit 2: The witness
1. The Witness. 2. Attendance of a Witness in a Court. 3. Duties of Witnesses. 4. Knowledge of the Subject on which Evidence is to be Given. 5. Behaviour in the Witness Box. 6. Duties of Witnesses. 7. General Impression

Study Unit 3: Process of giving evidence
1. Evidence. 2. The Admissibility of Evidence. 3. Guidelines for giving Verbal Evidence. 4. Behaviour in Situations which may Upset the Witness. 5. Refreshing of Memory. 6. Cross-examination of a Witness. 7. Common tactics used by Legal Practitioners to upset Witnesses. 8. Tactics of Cross-examination. 9. Use of Investigation Notes.

Study Unit 4: The court process
1. Charge Sheet. 2. The Trial. 3. Role of the Crime Investigation in the Judicial Process. 4. Operational Preparation. 5. Co-coordinating with the State Prosecutor. 6. Preparation of a Witness. 7. Support of the Officer during the Trial. 8. Preparation of the Officer's Evidence. 9. Testifying by the Officer.

Study Unit 5: Providing Feedback
1. Requirement for Feedback. 2. Information required During Feedback.
