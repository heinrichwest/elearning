Overview
Routine maintenance of office equipment is essential in maintaining productivity, ensuring the longevity of equipment, and preventing workflow disruptions. This course equips learners with the knowledge and practical skills required to plan, conduct, monitor, and document routine maintenance activities in accordance with organisational procedures, manufacturers’ instructions, and safety requirements. Learners will also understand how to liaise effectively with maintenance providers and uphold workplace standards.

Description
This course provides a structured introduction to the processes and responsibilities involved in conducting and managing routine maintenance of office equipment within a professional environment. Learners begin with an exploration of why routine maintenance is important, including benefits such as improved efficiency, reduced repair costs, enhanced safety, and minimised downtime. They will identify and list the office equipment requiring routine care—such as printers, copiers, telephones, projectors, and computer peripherals—and will explain the standard procedures used for maintaining each item according to organisational policies. Learners will also recognise the importance of notifying the appropriate personnel regarding upcoming maintenance to ensure transparency and continuity of operations.


Unit 1: Demonstrate an understanding of office equipment maintenance procedures and processes
1. The importance of conducting routine maintenance on office equipment is explained. 2. Office equipment for maintenance is identified and listed. 3. Procedures for maintenance of office equipment are explained. 4. Appropriate personnel are notified of impending maintenance.

Unit 2: Monitor and liaise with maintenance providers
1. Maintenance providers are identified, listed and contact details are obtained. 2. Suggestions and recommendations for suitable maintenance providers are submitted. 3. Maintenance agreements are studied and filed. 4. Maintenance providers are contacted to repair or maintain equipment where relevant. 5. Any problems with maintenance providers are identified and reported.

Unit 3: Conduct routine maintenance on designated office equipment
1. Type of routine maintenance is identified and explained. 2. Safety measures for routine maintenance are described. 3. Location of maintenance equipment and accessories are identified and procedure for retrieval is explained. 4. Routine maintenance is conducted according to manufacturers' instructions. 5. Office equipment is checked to ensure it is in working order before use. 6. Maintenance materials are disposed of in accordance with organisational requirements. 7. Routine maintenance is conducted within agreed time frames.
