Overview
In the dynamic world of automotive service, clear communication and systematic procedures are key to customer satisfaction and operational success. This course empowers learners with the knowledge and practical skills to efficiently process vehicle service or repair requests in line with organisational procedures and industry standards. From identifying customer concerns to securing approvals and managing service documentation, each module is designed to streamline service operations while maintaining high-quality customer interaction.

Description
This course provides a structured approach to managing vehicle service and repair requests within a professional automotive environment. Learners will explore how to determine and confirm customer concerns, retrieve and update service history, and communicate effectively with both clients and service teams. Emphasis is placed on managing documentation, handling warranties, compiling quotations, and ensuring all actions comply with company policies. The course also covers customer engagement in stressful situations, assessment of vehicle condition, and managing customer valuables. By course end, learners will be equipped to support seamless service delivery and maintain a high standard of customer care.


Unit 1: Establish customer concerns
1. Customer concerns are determined by using open-ended probing questions. 2. Customer concerns are confirmed by using closed directed questioning methods. 3. Service documentation is processed according to company procedures.

Unit 2: Use a customer's service history to ensure continuity of the service relationship
1. A customer's service history is retrieved from the service database according to standard operating procedures. 2. A customer's service information updated according to standard procedures. 3. The service history of the vehicle is referenced to ensure that service continuity is maintained.

Unit 3: Manage various pressured customer interface situations
1. The concerns and requirements of a customer are communicated to the service division as part of the service plan. 2. Calm, confident and positive communication is maintained with a client in the face of stressful situations. 3. Concerns and requirements of a customer are referred according to company policies and procedures. 4. Communication with a client and or other persons/divisions is recorded as per company policy and procedure.
Units 4: Sell service and repair options to customer
1. The systems and components identified in the vehicle for further servicing are described in accordance with company/manufacturers service policies. 2. A customer is advised on further requirements for safe motoring. 3. Quotations for servicing/repairs are compiled according to standard company procedures. 4. Approval for the further service or repair is obtained and agreed with a customer according to company policy and procedure.

1. Specific manufacturer's warranties and guarantees are described and explained in terms of terms and conditions. 2. Actions to be taken are explained in relation to warranty and guarantee documentation. 3. Warranty or guarantee actions are explained to a customer and processed according to company policy and procedures.

Unit 6: Conclude service consultancy process
1. The estimated time of collection is agreed with a customer in terms of workshop loads. 2. The existing condition and contents of the vehicle is assessed and noted in accordance with company procedures. 3. A job card is made out in accordance with company procedures. 4. Customer valuables are secured according to organisational policies and procedures.
