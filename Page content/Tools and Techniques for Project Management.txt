Overview
Effective project management depends on applying the right tools and techniques to meet project objectives. This course introduces learners to a range of proven methods that support planning, execution, monitoring, and problem-solving within projects. Through structured guidance and practical examples, learners will develop the ability to use these tools with confidence while recognising their benefits and limitations.

Description
This course provides a clear and practical introduction to project management tools and techniques, aligned with organisational standards and professional practice. Learners will explore a wide range of approaches, from planning and scheduling to monitoring progress and solving challenges as they arise. The course examines both the advantages and drawbacks of each tool, using case-based examples to show their application in real projects. Emphasis is placed on producing outputs that support individual, team, and organisational requirements, while encouraging critical thinking about how tools should be adapted to suit project needs.
Course Content



Duration:
3h 10m

Unit 1: Project management tools & techniques
1. A range of tools and techniques used on a project are identified and explained in accordance with project requirements. 2. Limitations and advantages of project management tools and techniques are explained using examples. 3. Examples of the usage of the project management tools and techniques are provided in a hard copy format.


Unit 2: Use a range of project management tools

1. A range of project management tools and techniques are used in accordance with established standards and procedures. 2. The output of project management tools and techniques meet the individual, team, and organisational needs/requirements.

Unit 3: Apply corrective action steps where project management tools and techniques usage problems occur
1. Problems with the usage of project management tools and techniques are identified against project requirements. 2. Possible solutions are identified and discussed in consultation with higher authority. 3. Authorised/agreed solutions are implemented according to the agreed steps.
