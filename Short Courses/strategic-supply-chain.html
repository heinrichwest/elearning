Strategic Supply Chain Management Concepts

Course Overview

Strategic supply chain management plays a vital role in enhancing corporate performance by aligning supply chain activities with broader business goals. This course provides learners with the knowledge and tools to analyse, develop, and implement supply chain strategies that support corporate strategy. Emphasising global competitiveness, innovation, and organisational alignment, the course prepares learners to make informed, strategic decisions that drive value and resilience across supply networks.

Duration

4 Hours 30 Minutes
Self-paced learning

Certification

All Levels


What You will Learn

  • Analyse the concept of strategy and corporate strategy development
  • Manage change processes directed towards achieving corporate strategy
  • Analyse strategic supply chain management contribution to corporate strategy
  • Evaluate concepts underlying strategic supply chain management
  • Assess the global supply market as a source of competitive advantage
  • Distinguish and assess various models of supply chain structures

Course Curriculum

  • Concepts related to corporate strategy are evaluated to determine the alignment between corporate strategy and the supply chain management strategy
  • The importance of the environment, resources and internal and external stakeholders is evaluated to determine their impact on the development of corporate strategy
  • The availability, development and allocation of resources to support corporate strategy are analysed in terms of their influence on the development and implementation of corporate strategy
  • The Corporate strategy process is reviewed in order to propose changes to improve corporate performance

  • Organisational instruments are developed to engender commitment to corporate strategy across functions, and at all levels of the organisation
  • The need for alignment between the component parts of an organisation are analysed to reflect their contribution to managing change
  • Resistance to change and strategic drift are diagnosed in order to address them through change management processes

  • Functional, business and corporate strategies are analysed to reflect their relationships with the integration of supply chain strategies into corporate strategy
  • Organisational structures and processes are analysed and aligned with strategic supply chain strategies
  • Innovative supply chain strategies are proposed to meet corporate and business targets

  • The validity of the supply chain concept is assessed both in terms of the nature of supply and the chain metaphor
  • Internal and external supply chain structures are analysed in order to link them with organisational and inter-organisational boundaries
  • The nature of supply chains is distinguished in private and public sectors
  • A variety of supply chains are analysed and compared in order to propose strategies to address them

  • The validity of the global supply market is assessed as a source of competitive advantage
  • The concept of lean is evaluated to determine the importance of the removal of waste from supply chains
  • The concept of agility is evaluated to determine the importance of responsiveness and efficiency of supply chains
  • The sources, nature and role of power are analysed to determine the influence on supply chains
  • The use of supply chains to deliver ethical and socio-economic outcomes alongside commercial ones is used to determine competitive advantage

  • The validity of the development of a preferred strategic supply chain model is assessed to provide best value for money and competitive advantage
  • The characteristics of adversarial and co-operative strategies are compared to reflect their appropriateness in different circumstances
  • Competence-based approaches for determining supply chain structures and relationships are applied for optimal supply chain operations
  • The supply model is assessed as a prerequisite for supply relationships
