
Overview
Successful project management and support within a Contact Centre environment depend on clear communication, collaboration, and proactive problem-solving. Through structured engagement with stakeholders and early identification of risks, participants will strengthen their ability to drive progress and uphold operational excellence.

Description
This course explores the essential processes for obtaining, evaluating, and analysing information critical to Contact Centre success. Learners will begin by identifying valid and reliable methods for sourcing project information and securing the co-operation of key stakeholders. They will then move on to providing timely, sensitive advice and support that empowers teams while maintaining independent work practices. A strong focus is placed on early detection of potential threats to success, ensuring learners are equipped to sustain momentum and contribute meaningfully to organisational goals.

Course Content 

Unit 1: Obtain and evaluate project information to facilitate contact centre problem solving
1. Valid and reliable methods and sources for obtaining project information are identified and agreed upon with relevant parties. 2. The cooperation of relevant parties in obtaining information is enlisted. 3. Available information from relevant parties is identified, obtained and evaluated in conjunction with higher authority. 4. Information gathered is analysed to identify potential improvement.

Unit 2: Provide advice and support regarding received information to facilitate problem solving
1. Advice and support are provided at appropriate times and only when necessary. 2. The methods of providing advice are sensitive to the personal needs and positions of the relevant individuals. 3. Advice and support are provided only so far as is necessary to allow progress to be maintained and to enable individuals and groups to work autonomously. 4. Threats to the contact centre's success are identified at an early stage.
