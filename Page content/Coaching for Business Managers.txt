Description
This course guides participants through the full cycle of selecting and coaching first-line managers, from defining performance criteria and conducting interviews to implementing a structured coaching process. Emphasis is placed on aligning individual performance with organisational goals through targeted feedback, development planning, and ongoing support. Learners will also be equipped to promote a coaching culture by empowering new managers to lead and develop their own teams effectively.


Unit 1: Selecting a firstline manager
1. (incl. 1.1) Selecting a first-line manager 2. Identification of key performance areas from a first-line manager's job profile 3. Understanding the requirements of the first-line manager position 4. Process of selecting candidates 5. Verification methods 6. Video assessing candidates against position requirements 7. Interview process 8. Making decisions and providing feedback to candidates

Unit 2: Planning the coaching process
1. (incl. 2.1) Planning the coaching process 2. Developing a coaching plan 3. Steps to take when developing a coaching plan 4. Preparing records of expected performance or performance standards 5. Creating a system for recording decisions and commitments before coaching

Unit 3: The coaching process
1. (incl 3.1 & 3.2) The coaching process 2. Role of a coach 3. The purpose, content and schedule of the coaching process 4. Conducting a coaching session 5. Elements of an effective coaching session 6. Coaching strategies 7. Coaching meeting checklist

Unit 4: Monitor and measure results of coaching sessions
1. (incl. 4.1 & 4.2) Monitor and measure results of coaching sessions 2. Guidelines for assessment 3. Giving feedback during and after coaching sessions 4. Giving positive feedback 5. Recording corrective action where requirements were not met 6. Conducting follow-up action
