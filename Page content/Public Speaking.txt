Supervisor: Communication and Interpersonal Skills
Overview
The art of crafting and delivering compelling speeches or presentations. This includes techniques for structuring content, engaging an audience, using effective body language and vocal variety, managing stage fright, and honing the skills needed to communicate confidently and persuasively in various public speaking situations.

Description
Knowing the benefits of public speaking is essential for individuals who want to become effective communicators, influential leaders, and confident presenters. It opens doors to numerous opportunities in various domains and contributes to personal, professional, and academic success.

Unit 1: What is public speaking?
1. Informative speaking. 2. Persuasive speaking.

Unit 2: Stage fright

Unit 3: Preparing your presentation
1. Before the speech preparation. 2. Select your material. 3. Construct an outline. 4. Write your draft. 5. Preparing your visuals. 6. Revise your speech. 7. Tie loose ends together. 8. Practise.

Unit 4: Presenting your talk
