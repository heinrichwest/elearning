Overview
Beyond mere collaboration lies the architecture of performance, where individuals align, adapt, and emerge as more than the sum of their parts. Structured meetings serve as the foundation of organisational decision-making, driving clarity, coordination, and action across teams and departments. (SACE endorsed 5 points)

Description
This course equips learners with the practical skills and theoretical foundations needed to plan, conduct, and evaluate structured meetings effectively. From preparing agendas and coordinating logistics to encouraging participation and managing differing viewpoints, the course emphasises clarity of communication and adherence to organisational protocols. Participants will also learn how to compile accurate meeting records, distribute actionable summaries, and continuously improve future meeting outcomes. Designed for both aspiring and current professionals, this course enhances organisational efficiency and fosters productive collaboration through well-executed meetings.


Unit 1: Prepare for a meeting
1. Physical arrangements and attendees required for a meeting are identified, and a checklist is prepared and actioned in terms of Standard Operating Procedures. 2. The purpose of an agenda, the expected outcomes of the meeting, the process of placing items on the agenda, and the roles of participants are explained in terms of Standard Operating Procedures. 3. The purpose of recording a meeting is explained according to Standard Operating Procedures. 4. Documents required for a meeting are checked for accuracy and completeness and distributed according to Standard Operating Procedures. 5. The minutes of the previous meeting were checked for accuracy and completeness.

Unit 2: Conduct a meeting
1. A meeting is conducted according to a pre-planned agenda and Standard Operating Procedures. 2. Opportunities are created to allow effective participation by people attending the meeting. 3. Discussions are summarised and recorded to indicate the proposed action, completion date, and person responsible.

Unit 3: Demonstrate techniques to deal with differing views during a meeting
1. Identify techniques to overcome potential lack of progress due to differing opinions during the meeting according to recognised theory and practice. 2. A technique is used to create progress in a meeting according to recognised theory and practice.

Unit 4: Distribute records for a meeting
1. Records are compiled in such a way to enable the decisions of a meeting to be implemented. 2. Records are checked to ensure that they are an accurate reflection, prior to distribution. 3. The meeting is reviewed and suggestions for improvement are made to enhance the effectiveness of future meetings.
