Protection of Personal Information Act (POPIA) Training

This course will help you understand the Protection of Personal Information Act (POPIA) and how to comply with it in your organization.

Sign Up Learn More

Course Overview

This POPIA training course helps you handle personal information with skill and confidence. You will learn the basic rules that make your organization compliant, from collecting and processing data to managing consent and handling data breaches. The course shows you how to implement a POPIA compliance framework from start to finish, setting clear policies, keeping data secure, and finishing strong. You'll gain the confidence to lead your organization's data protection efforts, big or small, and apply these steps at home or work, becoming a trusted data protection champion. This training provides the tools to grow your career and succeed in any job or industry.

What You'll Learn

  • Clearly define personal information and its importance.
  • Understand exactly how POPIA works in real-world scenarios.
  • Recognize different types of data and their structures.
  • Confidently apply simple tools and steps to manage personal information.
  • Identify essential tasks for successful POPIA compliance.

Course Curriculum

  • Explain the main features of POPIA, with easy examples.
  • Describe the basic concepts of personal information and data subjects.
  • Discuss why organizations need to comply with POPIA.
  • Explain different types of personal information.

  • Define the 8 conditions for lawful processing of personal information.
  • Describe and explain the major data processing principles.
  • Explain the differences between a responsible party and an operator.
  • Clarify the difference between a data subject and a responsible party.

  • Explain the reasons for protecting the rights of data subjects.
  • Illustrate the concept of consent, supported by an example.
  • Evaluate the purpose of allowing data subjects to access their personal information.
  • Define the concepts of data subject access requests in simple terms.

  • Explaining the role and powers of the Information Regulator.
  • Describe and explain the process of lodging a complaint with the Information Regulator.
  • Outline key responsibilities of the Information Officer.
  • Explain the concept of data breaches, providing examples of at least six different types.

  • Describe the key processes and activities involved in managing personal information from beginning to end.
  • Briefly describe the supplementary management sub-processes and activities that support the key data protection processes, and providing examples of each.
  • Explain the reasons for planning and controlling data protection, while using examples to illustrate the consequences of poor planning and lack of control.
